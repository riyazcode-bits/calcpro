Understanding Compound Interest

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and accumulated interest from previous periods. Albert Einstein called it "the eighth wonder of the world."

Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) + PMT √ó [((1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1) / (r/n)]

Key Concepts:

  • Principal: Your initial investment amount
  • Interest Rate: Annual percentage return
  • Compound Frequency: How often interest is calculated and added
  • Time: The longer you invest, the more powerful compounding becomes

Why Compounding Matters:

  • More frequent compounding = higher returns
  • Time is your biggest advantage - start early
  • Regular contributions dramatically increase final value
  • Even small differences in rate make huge long-term impact
