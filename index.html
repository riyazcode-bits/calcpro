How YouTube Earnings Work

CPM (Cost Per Mille) is the amount advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions on your videos. Your actual earnings depend on several factors:

  • Niche: Finance and business content typically earns $10-20 CPM, while entertainment earns $2-5 CPM
  • Audience Location: Viewers from US, UK, Canada generate higher CPMs than developing countries
  • Watch Time: Longer videos with higher retention earn more
  • Ad Types: Skippable ads, non-skippable ads, and mid-roll ads affect earnings
  • Seasonality: Q4 (Oct-Dec) typically has 30-50% higher CPMs due to holiday advertising

Note: This calculator provides estimates. Actual earnings vary based on many factors including viewer demographics, ad blockers, and YouTube's algorithm.

Tips to Increase Your YouTube Earnings

  • Create content in high-CPM niches (finance, tech, education, business)
  • Target audiences in countries with higher ad rates (US, UK, Canada, Australia)
  • Make videos over 8 minutes to enable mid-roll ads
  • Improve watch time and audience retention
  • Post consistently to grow your subscriber base
  • Optimize titles, thumbnails, and descriptions for SEO
